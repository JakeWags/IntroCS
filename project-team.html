Helen Hu (PI)

Helen manages the project and supervises the refinement of supplemental materials for POGIL activities. She travels to conferences to run POGIL workshops, recruits IntroCS instructors to the project, mentors teams, and shares the project’s results with the CS education community. Helen works closely with the Project Coordinator to help manage and coordinate the data collection for this grant.

Helen is a Professor of CS at Westminster College. She has authored multiple SIGCSE and ToCE publications on using POGIL in her IntroCS classes. She has facilitated POGIL workshops at national conferences.

Clifton Kussmaul (Co-PI)

Clifton supervises team recruiting and coordinates the regular meetings, helps to review and improve activities for this project, and will pilot IntroCS Python materials in his courses. He maintains the web site with materials and reporting functions, and plans and facilitates workshops for dissemination. He also serves as liaison to the national POGIL Project.

Clif is an Associate Professor of CS at Muhlenberg College. He was the PI for the CS-POGIL project and a co-PI for the OpenPath project. He has presented work on POGIL and led POGIL workshops at conferences and institutions in the US and India.

Chris Mayfield (Co-PI)

Chris supervises teams and mentors, including their asynchronous and synchronous meetings, their reflective logs, their activity feedback, and their community of practice. He is expanding and revising the project’s POGIL activities and travels to conferences to run POGIL workshops and help recruit instructors.

Chris is an Associate Professor of CS at James Madison University. He has authored several SIGCSE and TOCE publications on computational thinking in K- 12, database systems education, and POGIL. He is coauthor of the textbook Think Java: How to Think Like a Computer Scientist and the designer of JMU's CS 101 course.

Aman Yadav (Co-PI)

Aman leads the educational research in consultation with the other co-PIs. He is working closely with the co-PIs during all phases of the educational research including data collection, analysis, and writing up the findings.

Aman’s teaching and research focuses on computational thinking, computer science education, teacher preparation and problem-based learning. His work has been published in leading journals including ACM Transaction on Computing Education, Journal of Research in Science Teaching, Journal of Engineering Education, and Communications of the ACM.

Patricia B. Campbell (Evaluator)

Patricia heads the external evaluation team and has primary responsibility for the evaluation of the research study.

Formerly a professor at Georgia State University, Pat is President of Campbell- Kibler Associates (C-KA), Inc, an Association for Women in Science (AWIS) Fellow and has authored more than 100 publications. Pat has been involved in educational research and evaluation with a focus on science and mathematics education and issues of race/ethnicity, gender and disability since the mid 1970's. Her website, www.BeyondRigor.org, provides easy-to-use tips to improve the quality of evaluations and research done with diverse populations.

Debby Scharffs (Project Coordinator)

Debby serves as the project coordinator, keeping the project organized and on-track. She helps to track deliverables, activities, reimbursements, and stipends for the many project participants.

Debby joined Westminster College in 2014, working with the administration and faculty in the School of Arts & Sciences.
